My Portfolio

The portfolio tab of my website is where you can find a collection of my work and accomplishments. This page showcases some of the projects I have completed. You can browse through my projects to get a sense of my skills and interests, and learn more about the technologies I have worked with. I am constantly updating this page with new projects, so please check back often to see what I have been working on.

  • Number Cruncher

    A math quiz to learn basic math in an engaging way. Get highscores in addition, multiplication, and more. Click to find out more.

  • 3D Renderer using .NET

    I made this program to help me understand the basics of computer graphics. It takes in meshes of 3D objects and projects them onto a plane, i.e. what you are seeing.

  • Graphing Calculator

    This is a shameless clone of Desmos, a graphing calculator that can plot functions.

  • Podcast Website

    This is a website where you can listen to a podcast episode I was part in during the applied programming course at NTI Gymnasiet, Uppsala. It's a simple website with a clean design.

  • Hacking Simulator

    Have you ever wanted to be a Hollywood hacker? Well, I've got the perfect website for you! With this site, you can trick your non-technical friends into thinking you're hacking into the Mainframe. Just mash your keyboard and impress your friends.

  • The Train - My Train Blog

    I enjoy trains, so I made a blog for it. There are some shorter stories and some pictures of trains. I hope you enjoy it.

  • Pool With π

    There are many ways to calculate the value of pi. One of the most common methods for calculating pi is to use the infinite series known as the Leibniz formula but thats pretty boring. This site simulates the elastic collision between three bodies, and the total number of collisions estimate pi.

  • Bézier Curve

    This site shows you how bézier curves are made with lerps, linear interpolations.
